Who am I?

Hi! My name is Kayla Harwell. I am a rising junior from Howard Univerity. I am 19 years old and I am from Buffalo, NY. I didn't plan on majoring in CS until I was a junior in HS. Before then I had my sights set on being an oncologist, but after a few medical internships I realized that wasn't for me and shifted my focus towards technology,something that I'd always had a love for.

At Howard I like to spend my time involved on campus through clubs like NSBE and holding E-board positions in ACM and SWE.I love Howard and its culture. Everyone there is unique and I would'nt be where I am today without them. Along with this oppertunity Howard has opened many doors for me to grow and learn.

What Projects Have I Worked On?

  • "Absent Future" - This project is my most recent. It is a first person shooter game about the horrors of a future affected by global warming. This was made my friends and I during an Amazon Games hackathon using Lumberyard, Amazon's game engine. My specific role was the back end developer, I was responsible for making the computer generated AI enemies run towards the player and if one touched the player, one life would be lost. We ended up winning the grand prize with this project. This was the first hackathon we ever won, so this project is near and dear to my heart.
  • "Naturally U"- This project is something I'm proud of because it's all about natural hair, something that I love about myself and know a lot about. Naturally U is a all-in-one resource for natural hair.It shows you affordable hair products for your hair type, style tutorials, and the closest barbers or hair stylists to the user. Naturally U was made during the UNCF CS Summer Academy where I worked with a team on 3 to complete this project using HTML,CSS,and Python.My specific role was the full stack developer. I worked on the front end of several pages of the app including the home page and the tutorial selection page and on the back end of the login page. At the end of the summer pitch competition our project took the grand prize!
  • "Little Green Foot"- This project is a sort of environmental effect tracker. In this app you could mark "environmentally friendly" actions you did that day such as carpooling to work, recycling, etc. and you would earn points from those actions which the user could cash out those points in the form of gift cards and other prizes. My role on this project was the full stack developer. I worked with the front end developer to create all the visuals for the mock up and I worked with the backend developer to create the point system algorithm.
